Part 3 · Resourcing Model

Govern & enable at the center — execute at the edge

A deliberately three-tier hub-and-spoke GCC/GBS model. Dataiku: companies that have scaled AI are 3× more likely to use a hub-and-spoke structure. Bars below are live from the simulation — watch the pooled bench move into contention as demand rises.

RACI logic of the tiers

Hub is Accountable for standards, platform, certification and the semantic layer; Consulted on individual products. Spokes are Accountable for their data products, backlog, adoption and local value — Responsible for build within guardrails. The pooled bench flexes on demand. When contention repeatedly breaches SLAs, the operating-model response is to convert a pooled role into a dedicated spoke seat — visible on the Mission Control alerts. Best practice is a single Accountable per decision; Dataiku warns against centralizing every product decision in staggered committees.

The Spine of the Model

The certified semantic / metrics layer

Where yield, conversion cost/tonne, OTIF and gross margin are defined once and served consistently to every dashboard, spreadsheet and AI agent. This is what separates a governed CoE from a report factory. Atlan: in the 61% of enterprises running 4+ BI platforms, it is the only mechanism that stops the same metric returning different numbers in different tools.

Part 4 · Value Realization Office

Value is harvested — not assumed

Most analytics value leaks. McKinsey Global Institute: only 10–40% of achievable analytics value is captured (manufacturing 20–30%) — implying 60–90% leakage without disciplined realization. The VRO is a separate governed function, co-owned with Finance, with a baseline and named benefit owner set at intake. Profit.co: the top failure is defining benefits after approval, once baselines are already compromised.

Value trees — operational KPI → financial outcome (steel)

Value evaporation is a real failure state. Skip the VRO and go-live without a baseline, and realized value collapses toward the McKinsey leakage floor — the ledger keeps claiming, but Finance can't reconcile it. Toggle VRO discipline off on Mission Control to watch the realized-vs-claimed gap tear open in real time. This is the single most compelling narrative for a finance-transformation pitch.

Part 2 · People, Skillsets & Decision Rights

Who's involved — and who decides

The CoE works only when the demand side (the business raising the need) and the supply side (the CoE) are wired together under a shared governance spine, with scarce skills pooled across both.

Read the shift across the row: the business is Accountable at Intake (they own the problem) and again at Operate (they own the realized benefit). The CoE Product Owner carries Accountability through the build; Data Governance is Accountable at Data Enablement and Publish — where a KPI becomes certified and trustworthy. Value Realization / Finance is Accountable only at the end, when claimed value is reconciled to actuals.

The pivot role is the Analytics Translator. Most CoEs fail not on tooling but on the seam between the two sides — the business can't spec what it needs, and the CoE builds what was asked rather than what was meant. The translator, product owner and BRM are the connective tissue; leadership owns the portfolio and the value, and the business sponsor — not the CoE — remains the accountable benefit owner once a KPI goes live.
